SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY: EVERYBODY'S BUSINESS A FORUM

Reykjavik, Iceland
1st – 2nd June 2019

The field of addictive behaviours has long been characterised by its wide spectrum of interests. Producers, suppliers, consumer groups, governments, heath agencies, NGOs, individuals with and without consumption-related problems, all have individualised and often conflicting views and attitudes to production, marketing, distribution, taxation, harm reduction and legality of consumption in any given set of circumstances.

The often-conflicting beliefs and attitudes of these various interest groups and persons have led through time to a situation in which communication between bodies with a shared concern to minimise social harm and promote positive health has become at best stilted, frequently non-existent and occasionally confrontational.

In October 2017 ICAA convened an invitational "Think tank" on Civil Society and NGO’s in the field of addiction (Porto 2017). Following two days of challenging and productive dialogue it was agreed that an open Forum should be arranged to address the nature of and potential for dialogue

On the 1st and 2nd June 2019, in Reykjavik, Iceland, a Forum will take place to explore the issue of Social Responsibility in the field of addictive behaviours.

Drawing on the experiences of leaders in the fields of social responsibility, political change, addictive behaviours, health promotion and of research in societal behaviours this Forum will first seek to explore and define the key societal challenges facing stakeholders in the fields of substance use, gambling and communication technology. Secondly the Forum will examine the means of establishing productive and ongoing dialogue between stakeholder bodies.

The core themes of this two day event will be:

  1. Changing dialogue:
    • What are the factors which act to limit or facilitate dialogue between stakeholders
    • What has been the contribution of governments to changing the business model for prevention and intervention ?
    • What is the relationship between government, economic factors, and quality in shaping responses to addictive behaviours
    • How do we change the way people think about these topics?
    • How do we, as diverse stakeholders, work together for the public good?.
  2. Disruptors, influencers, and chain reactions
    • The role and value of disruptors in bringing about change.
    • Who are the people who influence policy and strategy
    • What can we learn from chain reactions in policy and behaviours
  3. From Silos to Synergy.
    • What are the positions of SROs? NGOs? Governments? Policy-makers? Users?
    • What do stakeholders want
    • What do stakeholders really need
    • What mechanisms will underpin a continuing and productive dialogue

In deciding to locate this exciting event in Reykjavik the Organising Committee has taken particular note of Iceland’s success in recent years in achieving significant reductions in substance use and associated problems among young people. Social and legislative intervention aimed at fostering teen wellbeing and family cohesion under the overarching national plan called Youth in Iceland has proved an outstanding success. and will provide an inspiring background against which the Forum will explore the benefits to be gained from pursuing common goals through cooperative working.

The Forum will provide an environment in which participants seek to understand each other’s position and to focus on the positive contributions to be made by all to the general good.

In a field too often characterised by conflict this Forum will demonstrate what can be achieved by a "coalition of the willing"
